The Web in the Early 2000's

In the early 2000s, access of broadband internet like DSL and cable, made it possible for users to get on the internet at much faster speeds. This advancement allowed for the development of new market with bandwidth-intensive applications such as streaming video and online gaming.

Web 2.0

The Term “Web 2.0” was established in 2004 by the founder of O'Reilly Media, Tim O'Reilly. The term is used to describe a shift to more user-generated content on the internet which was just starting to take place.

Social media platforms and collaborative platforms are used more frequently and create a sens of an online community

Durring the early 2000’s, more business oriented e-commerce and online marketplaces started to rise in popularity, with Amazon and Ebay being some of the platforms that were dominating the scene

Web 2.0 is a period in the webs history where engaging with the web became a more active experience for its users, and it really shows how the web was starting to form into the tool that we use today.
